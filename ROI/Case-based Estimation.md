### Supermarket Margin
Assume Supermarket Margin is about $ **M**.

### Net Profit Margin
The net profit margin for food retail businesses is about 1%~3%<sup>[1] [2]</sup>.

    e.g. From the balance sheet of Albertsons (Albertsons Companies, Inc. and Subsidiaries Consolidated Statements of Operations and Comprehensive Income (in millions, except per share data)), 

we can calculate the net profit margin for 2024, 2023, 2022 is 1296/79237.7=1.64%, 1513.8/77649.7=1.95%, 1625.1/71887.0=2.26%<sup>[3]</sup>.

Thus, the net profit margin range: 1%~3% is acceptable.

### Improved Net Profit for Blockchain Project
Carrefour reported a 2.7% like-for-like sales growth in Q1 2024. Assuming similar performance for subsequent quarters, this corresponds to an annual uplift of roughly 1%~3% on a comparable basis<sup>[4]</sup>.

#### Conclusion
ΔNet Profit= M × (1%−3%) × (1%−3%) = M/10000 ~ 9M/10000

### Costs
Implementation Costs<sup>[5]</sup>
- Infrastructure Setup: C<sub>1</sub>
- Development Costs: C<sub>2</sub>
- Integration Expenses: C<sub>3</sub>
- Training and Support: C<sub>4</sub>
- Maintenance (Annual): C<sub>5</sub>

### ROI
**Initial Investment** = Infrastructure Setup + Development + Integration + Training & Support = C<sub>1</sub> + C<sub>2</sub> + C<sub>3</sub> + C<sub>4</sub>

**Annual Net Cash Flow** = Δ Net Profit – Maintenance Cost = (M/10000 ~ 9M/10000) - C<sub>5</sub>

**ROI** = Annual Net Cash Flow / Initial Investment ×100% = $\frac{(M/10000 ~ 9M/10000) - C_5} {C_1 + C_2 + C_3 + C_4}$ x 100%

**Payback**  = $\frac {Initial Investment}{(1+r)^t} $ >= Annual Net Cash Flow 

#### Interest Rate
r = r<sub>f</sub> + π + ρ 
- r<sub>f</sub>: Risk-free Interest Rate<sup>[6]</sup>
- π: Inflation Rate<sup>[7]</sup>
- ρ: Risk Premium<sup>[8]</sup>


**Payback**  = $ \text{Payback} = \frac{(M/10000 \sim 9M/10000) - C_5}{(1+r_f + \pi + \rho)^t} \ge C_1 + C_2 + C_3 + C_4 $
